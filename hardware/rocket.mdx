## Overview

The MANPADS rocket is a folding-fin guided rocket with a 3D-printed body designed in Fusion 360. An ESP32 DevKit V1 acts as the onboard flight computer, using an MPU6050 IMU and four canard servos to stabilize roll during flight.

CAD files (Fusion 360 archive format):

- `CAD Files/Folding Rocket.f3z` — full rocket body with folding fin mechanism
- `CAD Files/Rocket Nozzle.f3z` — motor nozzle assembly

## Electronics

All components connect to the ESP32 DevKit V1 flight computer.

| Component | GPIO / Interface | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ESP32 DevKit V1 | — | Flight computer |
| MPU6050 IMU | SDA=21, SCL=22 | I2C, address 0x68 |
| Left canard servo | GPIO 26 | Center 115°, max deflection ±12° |
| Right canard servo | GPIO 25 | Center 80°, max deflection ±12° |
| Up canard servo | GPIO 27 | Center 80°, max deflection ±12° |
| Down canard servo | GPIO 14 | Center 115°, max deflection ±12° |
| Ignition servo | GPIO 5 | ON=150°, OFF=35° |
| UART to launcher | TX2=GPIO 17, RX2=GPIO 16 | Serial2 at 115200 baud |

<Note>
The servo pulse range is 500–2400 µs (set via `attach(pin, 500, 2400)`). Use this range when replacing servos to ensure full travel is available.
</Note>

## Canard stabilization

The rocket uses a folding-fin body for compact storage at launch. Four canard fins near the nose provide active roll stabilization during flight.

All four canards move together by the same offset amount each control cycle. The PD controller computes:

```
servo_offset = constrain(Kp × roll + Kd × rate, -12, 12)
```

Where:
- `roll` — integrated roll angle in degrees (from gyroscope)
- `rate` — gyroscope angular rate in degrees/second
- `Kp`, `Kd` — proportional and derivative gains (defaults: Kp=0.5, Kd=0.2)
- Output is clamped to ±12° maximum deflection

Each servo writes `center ± servo_offset`. The canards only actuate while in the `FLIGHT` state; they return to center during `IDLE` and `ARMED`.

<Tip>
PID gains can be updated in flight via the dashboard using the `PID,<Kp>,<Kd>` UDP command relayed through the launcher.
</Tip>

## Ignition sequence

When the launcher sends the `IGNITE` command over UART:

1. The rocket enters the `IGNITING` state.
2. The ignition servo moves to **150°** (ON position), activating the igniter.
3. After **2500 ms**, the ignition servo returns to **35°** (OFF position).
4. The rocket transitions to the `FLIGHT` state and sends `IGNITED` back to the launcher.
5. The canard servos begin active stabilization.

<Warning>
The ignition servo drives a physical igniter. Ensure the rocket is secured in the launcher and the area is clear before arming.
</Warning>

## Assembly

<Steps>
  <Step title="Print the body parts">
    Print all parts from `CAD Files/Folding Rocket.f3z` and `CAD Files/Rocket Nozzle.f3z`. Parts were designed for FDM printing. Use a material rated for heat near the nozzle section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the folding fin mechanism">
    The four tail fins fold inward for storage inside the launch tube. Assemble the spring-loaded hinge at the base of each fin and verify that the fins snap fully open and lock when released.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mount the canard servos">
    Press-fit or screw the four SG90-class micro servos into the canard bay near the nose. Route servo cables back through the body to the ESP32.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the ignition servo">
    Mount the ignition servo (GPIO 5) in the motor bay. Connect its arm to the igniter trigger mechanism. Verify it moves freely between 35° (safe) and 150° (fire).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the ESP32 and MPU6050">
    Secure the ESP32 DevKit V1 and MPU6050 breakout in the avionics bay. Connect the MPU6050 to SDA=GPIO 21 and SCL=GPIO 22. Orient the IMU so the roll axis aligns with the rocket's long axis.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wire the UART link">
    Run a two-wire UART cable from TX2 (GPIO 17) and RX2 (GPIO 16) to the rear connector that mates with the launcher umbilical.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Power-on and calibrate">
    After wiring, power the flight computer. On boot it runs a 200-sample gyro calibration to zero the offset and measure the physical skew angle. Re-run calibration with the `calibrate` command from the dashboard if the rocket is repositioned.
  </Step>
</Steps>
