title: "Launch procedure"
description: Step-by-step checklist for a safe and successful launch of the MANPADS rocket system.
---

This page covers the full launch sequence from power-on to ignition confirmation. Complete every step in order and do not skip the pre-launch checks.

---

## Pre-launch checks

Verify all of the following before proceeding:

- Rocket flight computer is flashed and powered
- Launcher ground station is flashed and powered
- Arm switch is in the **SAFE** position (GPIO5 HIGH)
- Rocket is seated in the launcher tube with the igniter installed
- GPS module is connected — the launcher halts with a repeating error tone at boot if no NMEA data is detected within 5 seconds

## Dashboard connection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect to the launcher WiFi">
    On your laptop, connect to the `ROCKET_LAUNCHER` WiFi network (password: `launch_secure`).

    | Parameter | Value |
    |-----------|-------|
    | SSID | `ROCKET_LAUNCHER` |
    | Launcher IP | `192.168.4.1` |
    | UDP port | `4444` |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the dashboard">
    From the `Firmware/` directory:

    ```bash
    python dashboard.py
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm connection">
    Wait for the status label to change from **"Connecting to Launcher AP..."** to **"Connected: 192.168.4.1"**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check GPS status">
    Verify the GPS bubble color in the **Environment & Location** panel:

    | Color | Meaning | Action |
    |-------|---------|--------|
    | Green | Fix acquired | Proceed |
    | Orange | Searching for satellites | OK to proceed |
    | Red | No GPS module | Do not proceed |
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Gyro calibration

Before arming, calibrate the gyro with the rocket stationary in the tube:

1. Click **CALIBRATE GYRO** in the dashboard Mission Control panel.
2. Confirm the dialog prompt ("Keep rocket STILL. Zeroing Gyro.").
3. Keep the rocket completely still for approximately 1 second (200 samples at 5 ms each).
4. Verify that the **Roll** readout in the live telemetry panel shows a value near `0.0°`.

<Note>
  Gyro calibration is also triggered automatically during the arming sequence when the launcher sends `ARM` to the rocket. Repeating it manually before arming gives you an early check that the IMU is responding.
</Note>

## Arming sequence

Flip the arm switch from SAFE to ARMED to start the arming sequence. The following happens automatically:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launcher enters ARMING state">
    The arm switch (GPIO5) transitions from HIGH to LOW. The launcher moves the launcher servo to the ON position (170°) and begins waiting for a `READY` message from the rocket.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rocket responds READY">
    The rocket firmware sends `READY` over Serial2 every 1 second while in `IDLE` state. The launcher waits up to **8 seconds** for this message. If no `READY` is received, the launcher calls `abortSequence()`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="3 rapid beeps and orientation reference">
    On receipt of `READY`, the launcher plays 3 rapid beeps and reads the MPU6050 accelerometer to record the launcher's orientation reference (`cal_ay`, `cal_az`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launcher sends ARM">
    The launcher sends `ARM` to the rocket over Serial2. The rocket calibrates its gyro and transitions from `IDLE` to `ARMED`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launcher enters READY state">
    The launcher transitions to `READY`, turns the LED on solid, and plays a success tone (2 beeps, 150 ms each).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dashboard updates">
    The state dot in the dashboard Mission Control panel turns **yellow** (ARMED).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Do not move the rocket during the arming sequence. The launcher reads the MPU6050 orientation reference immediately after receiving `READY`. Movement during this window will produce an inaccurate reference angle.
</Warning>

## Ignition

Two methods are available. Use the physical button as the primary method.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Physical button">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Hold the launch button">
        Hold the launch button (GPIO18) continuously. The buzzer sounds for the entire hold duration.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Hold for 1 full second">
        Keep the button held. The button must remain pressed for at least **1 second** before ignition triggers. Releasing early cancels the sequence.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Launcher sends ignition commands">
        After the 1-second hold, the launcher sends `CALIBRATE` then `IGNITE` to the rocket over Serial2 (20 ms apart).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Rocket ignition servo fires">
        The rocket's ignition servo moves to ON (150°). After **2500 ms**, the servo returns to OFF (35°) and the rocket transitions to `FLIGHT`.
      </Step>
      <Step title="IGNITED ACK received">
        The rocket sends `IGNITED` back to the launcher. The launcher turns off the LED.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Dashboard updates">
        The state dot turns **green** (FLIGHT).
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Digital launch (dashboard)">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Click DIGITAL LAUNCH">
        Click the **DIGITAL LAUNCH** button in the Mission Control panel.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm the warning dialog">
        A confirmation dialog appears: **"This bypasses the physical launch button."** Click OK to proceed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Launch command sent">
        The dashboard sends a `launch` UDP packet to the launcher at `192.168.4.1:4444`. The launcher sets `udpLaunchTriggered = true` and proceeds with the same ignition sequence as the physical button method.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
      Digital launch bypasses the physical 1-second button hold. Ensure the launch area is clear and the system is fully armed before clicking this button.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Post-launch

- Monitor the **Plot** panel in the dashboard for roll angle and servo response during flight.
- The launcher LED turns off after ignition is confirmed.
- Flip the arm switch back to **SAFE** to reset the system. The launcher loops in `waitForSwitchReset()` until the switch returns to SAFE before accepting new commands.

<Note>
  After ignition, the system will not accept a new arming sequence until the arm switch has been returned to SAFE. This applies whether ignition succeeded or `abortSequence()` was called.
</Note>
